Hub & Spoke Strategy

Our content strategy prioritizes creating pages that prospects want & Google loves. the goal is to strike that perfect balance between satisfying the algorithm while attracting buyers.

Google doesn't rank pages individually

Counter to what most people believe about SEO, Google doesn't rank pages individually. They rank pages based on clusters of information within your site.

Google will analyze your entire site when considering where to rank even a single page. That's why it's so important to consider how other pages can work with the page you're trying to rank.

A Hub

is a page that is created to rank for a highly desirable keyword. One with thousands of searches per month, high competition, and significant buying intent from the audience.

These pages are the most complex and detailed pages on your site, they have to be to compete in search engines.

But making a great pages still isn't enough to earn a top ranking... but that's where Spokes come in.

A Spoke

is a page that is created to support the rankability of a hub. Generally a subtopic or a unique way of approaching the topic covered in the Hub.

Reach a top ranking

Here's an example targeting the term Project Management Software:

Hub =

Everything You Need to Know About Project Management Software

Spoke =

How Much Does Project Management Software Cost?

Reach a top ranking

In real application, every Hub we create has 8+ spokes created to support it.

This insures a rich network of content not only ranking for the primary term but also many smaller niche terms that also attract & convert relevant buyers.

Answers to our most frequently asked questions

What is SEO?

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of building your website, content, and code-base to support search engines processes for indexing and distributing pages. The most common search engine being Google, most SEO is focused on adhering to Google's ongoing algorithm adjustments to appear higher on the Search Engine Ranking Page (SERP).

What is on-page SEO vs off-page SEO?

On-Page SEO refers to optimizations done to a pages content. These could be changes related to text, images, layout, links, and more. Off-Page SEO refers to optimizations done outside of the primary website. This could refer to backlink generation campaigns such as PR mentions, directory listings, and other forms of digital partnerships.

How long does SEO take?

Historically SEO has always been regarded as taking a long time. Common timelines provided by SEO agencies is 6-12 months to show growth. However in our experience at 2POINT we measure in quarters and expect to see client results in the first 90 days. For websites that don't have a history of optimization we can often see growth in the first 45 days.

How do you choose an SEO agency?

We recommend taking your agency hiring decisions very seriously. An agency is a partner for your business that will accelerate or hinder growth if you don't have the right one. Vet them carefully, read reviews, read content that they've published, see how long they've been in the industry, and evaluate pricing. Be cautious about cheap work, great experts are expensive.

How much does SEO cost?

SEO costs can vary widely and are mostly impacted by your goals. In general we price our services with the goal that you recieve 3-4x your investment in the short term and an uncalculatable ROI in the long term. With a small investment between $3000-$5000/month you can expect steady growth in search rankings, to hit large and agressive goals you will likely be recommended a custom pricing structure.
